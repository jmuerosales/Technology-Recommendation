THINGS TO CONSIDER WHEN BUYING A TELEVISION OR MONITORS:

Step 1. Purpose

When buying a screen, we should consider on what we would like to do with it. For example, if we want it to be used as a television for our living room as we watch things in a comfortable position, then we would have to consider the size and where we would place it.

Step 2. Specifications

When looking for the right monitor, we should first research on what is suitable and for the price we would like to buy it for. We should also consider reading online reviews, reccomendations and watching promotions in order to choose the best that would suit for our wants and needs. Different brands also offers different strengths and weaknesses in their products. Make sure to find the most suitable for your use.

  • Connection Ports

    • As mentioned in the first step, we should find out what is the purpose of our screen would be. Therefore, we should consider asking if the port is available on the said screen. Usually screens have HDMI ports but it has different forms and uses as newer versions mostly uses the higher version of ports. We should also consider what kind of USB port is our television supporting.

  • Screen Resolution

    • Television this days that are being sold is usually at full HD (1980x1080) or 1080p at minimum. We should consider also what kind of screen will be your tv would have: LCD, LED, AMOLED. This would create differences in colors, brightness, contrast etc. Keep in mind that the higher the resolution, usually the more expensive it is.

  • Refresh Rate

    • If the primary use of the screen is for gaming, we should look for those with higher refresh rates as it may affect your gaming experience. The lower the refresh rate, the slower and more blurry your video/ graphic quality would be. If you are using Tv's for casual reasons, then go for higher resolutions instead.

  • Power Consumption

    • Of course it is important! Aside from saving money with the less and more effective use of electricity, we would be saving our environment as there would be less power consumption with it. We should take a look at how many Kilowatt per hour and how much would that increase the cost of our electricity.
